Take-Aim

Take-Aim was PointBlanc's pre-event. It was conducted on 23rd September from 20:00:00 to 23:30:00.

Gallery

     Take Aim, PointBlanc’s pre Aether event, was hosted on Monday, 23rd September 2019. Take Aim was an intra college eSports event aimed at propelling gaming culture, competitiveness and introducing people unfamiliar with the gaming world to witness this.

     The preparations would start right as the idea was conceived. One week of discussions, one night to make it all stand. We got tables and benches for a premier eSports feel, decorated the place with LED Lights and strips to create an aura of RGB and of course had our very own custom built PC act as the main spectator/operator PC (called Winry) .

     The day began in a flurry of arrangements and logistical work. Once everything was set, we were ready to kick off. We had a packed up room with about 150 students sitting eagerly. Some were here to cheer their friends, some were here to try out games and of course some were here to compete for glory. Begin.

     We started the gaming event with the comeback-of-the-year game. Minecraft. For Minecraft our goal was simple, let’s showcase the Ender Dragon being beat to a pulp by our elite group of students who had their enchantments and gear ready for the night. 15 of them went into the dark realm of the End. 7 of them perished, the rest reclaimed victorious and came out with the egg defining themselves as the rulers of the seed. After the End Battle, to spice up the competition, we had our contestants on the stage battle it out in Minecraft Hunger Games. 17 participants playing it live, slowly and slowly one after the other they kept dying to each other until the last one surviving was Theabominog declaring himself as #1!

     As the Minecraft matches were going on and being broadcasted and commentated on the projectors, we had a PUBG tournament going on at the back of the room as well. After an intense 28 minutes of Battle, Team Damn (Damn Dez, Damn Angry, Damn Gomel and CIA MrAli) came out securing themselves a chicken dinner and a massive round of applause.

     After Minecraft, we had our showboat event, the Paladins invitational. But before we get into that, let’s talk a little bit about the scenario at hand. Minecraft and PUBG just got over and Paladins had a few connections issues to sort out, so what do we do in the downtime to keep people engaged. We had laptops set up with nostalgic games like Tekken, Cuphead, NFS and VR experiences like SuperHot VR and Beat Saber for people to try out and enjoy.

     Once Paladins was set up, the game was on. We invited the biggest Paladins rivalry in our college to partake at this stage. Team KK vs Team BSB. This rivalry had been going on for two years now and the pregame trash talk was fresh. Game 1 began with a flurry of cheers and excitement from the crowd. Each team had their supporters cheering for them when a play happened. The game was a flurry of emotions and suffice to say that Paladins is one of the most fun eSports games to watch. Everyone in the room was hooked, whether they knew what they were looking at or not.

     Game 1 turned out to be a spectacular roller coaster of emotions. With both teams at 1 point each, Team BSB decided that they had enough playing small and went all on board the payload for the next rounds managing a 1-3 lead and requiring just another capture to win. However, the beaten in morale Team KK had a mental ace up their sleeves, they knew they had to step up for their glory and put up a fight of equal consequence on a stage like this. They did just that by winning the next two rounds and tying the score at 3-3. With the last round left and winning momentum favoring Team KK, they were able to successfully suppress Team BSB and take Game 1 for themselves.

     Game 2 was an evenly spaced match between these two competitive teams, the snipes from BSB were spot on but so were the heals from KK nullifying them. Every attack was checked by a defending opponent and every role was filled in. You could literally pick anyone to spectate and you would just be in awe at their skill and quick decision making. The fans were on the side of BSB, cheering them on. BSB made tons of important picks during the game, wreaking havoc to KK who were ever so effectively trying to hold back their attacks or push their defenses. A decisive Game indeed that had to go to BSB leading to a game 3 to break the tie.

     This is where it would end. The next 15-20 minutes would decide who’d walk away as the winners. Sweat dripped from the players, and discussions about tactics and pick select were hot. Their serious faces were on and there was no time to joke about. Well, at least that’s what both the teams were thinking but I guess one of these teams took it way to seriously. I mean, they were unstoppable. The game went in early favor of BSB scoring the first point. However KK had just kept quietly staring down the arena with their beast locked up. Now they felt like releasing it, and ever since, round after round KK kept winning and the positive momentum from them and the fans kept pulling them ahead securing their victory decisively and crowning themselves as the Paladins team of the college. Team KK were comprised of (yourdeathgod, sandeepsayi, virtualsailor, kk (Captain), vsvsharma)

     At the same time, CSGO and FIFA qualifiers were going on. The CSGO semis were being played to decide the second finalists. Team Yes ended up winning the semis in a close decisive 14-16. Team Yes would thus face off against the invincible Team pi in a best of finals.

     As the CSGO game was being set up, the FIFA finalists were decided. Rohan Joseph beat the defending champion, Yashwant Thota in a close 2-1 game and secured his spot in the finals meanwhile Om Goyal beat Jansen Khan to count himself as the challenger. The game was intense, with Om picking up and early lead right before the half time only to be put in his place by Rohan equalizing soon after the half time whistle. After that, it was all Rohan, skill moves, tricks, possession. He forced Om to retreat and play defensively meanwhile making it a nuisance for himself cause Om hadn’t gotten this far just by his attack tactics, his defense was spot on. However, Rohan lead Om into an attacking trap and used the opportunity to create a counter which turned out to be a skill shot goal, during the goalie and chipping it over to end up in the box and taking the lead. The video below is the reactions of that. We really wish we could have got the play :(


     The crowd couldn’t keep to their seats and everyone roared at the spectacle ahead. To solidify his lead, Rohan scored another and just like that won the entire tournament becoming the best FIFA player in the college.

     CSGO was the last of our games, the invincible Team pi defeated Team yes in a awestruck battle. 16-4 the scoreline lay as Beast and NeXT from Team pi held on to their AWPs and never let an opponent target out of sight. With quick comms and insightful positioning, their communication would show from their plays and one after another they kept winning to stand invincible and strong as the undefeated CSGO team of the college. Team pi consists of (NeXT, Beast, KaiserPanic, Ashura, YSA001)

     All in all, this event was as close as it got to having a large scale intercollege eSports event in our college and taught us a lot about managing, handling crowds, delivering impromptu, and dealing with technical and managerial issues. The feedback was amazing with students wanting more to happen in the same semester. Some wanting even every week. This is what excites us to bring this to Aether this year for the entire city.

-Makflakes
